Energy Intro lesson

Energy Types and Conversions

A short introduction to identifying energy stores and showing how energy is transferred from one form to another.

Lesson objective: Be able to draw simple energy conversion diagrams for everyday devices.
Big idea

Energy is not created or destroyed.

It is transferred between stores and pathways, often with some energy ending up in less useful forms such as thermal energy or sound.

Slide 3 Method

How to draw an energy conversion diagram

  • Start with the input energy.
  • Show the useful output.
  • Add any wasted outputs such as thermal energy or sound.
  • Keep it simple and label each part clearly.
General structure
Input energy
→
Useful output
+
Wasted output(s)

Not every device has the same useful output, but most real devices also produce some unwanted thermal energy or sound.

Slide 5 Thinking carefully

Useful or wasted?

The answer depends on what the device is for.

  • For a hairdryer, thermal energy is useful.
  • For a light bulb, thermal energy is mostly wasted.
  • For a vacuum cleaner, kinetic energy and sound are not equally useful.
Good habit

Always ask:

What is the device meant to do?
That tells you which output is useful and which outputs are wasted.
